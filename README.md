Sasol Limited Financial Analysis and Valuation
==============================================

## Overview

This repository showcases a full fundamental analysis and equity valuation of Sasol Limited (JSE: SOL), an integrated energy and chemicals company headquartered in South Africa. The project combines a written equity research–style report with an Excel-based financial model to assess Sasol’s performance, risk profile, and intrinsic value using historical data from 2020–2024.[11][12]

## Project Structure

- `ESEC-Assigniment-repor.docx`  
  Equity research report covering company overview, segment analysis, macro drivers, strategic transformation (Sasol 2.0 and “Future Sasol”), ESG profile, and investment thesis.[11]

- `My-financial-cashflow.xlsx`  
  Multi-sheet financial model including:  
  - Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement, and OCI (SOIC, SOFP, SOCF, SOCI).[12]
  - Derived financial ratios (liquidity, profitability, leverage, efficiency, shareholder metrics, growth).[12]
  - Cash flow diagnostics and coverage metrics.[12]
  - DCF valuation using FCFF, WACC, terminal growth, and sensitivity to key drivers.[12]

## Objectives

- Analyse Sasol’s historical financial performance (2020–2024) based on audited statements.[12]
- Diagnose liquidity, solvency, profitability, and operating efficiency using a comprehensive ratio set.[12]
- Evaluate capital structure, cash flow adequacy, and leverage sustainability.[12]
- Value Sasol using a multi-stage FCFF DCF and validate with a Gordon Growth model on levered free cash flow.[12]
- Translate quantitative findings into a coherent equity story and recommendation.[11][12]

## Data and Methodology

- **Data sources:** Sasol Group consolidated financial statements and market data exports (Capital IQ-style) for 2020–2024.[12]
- **Financial analysis:**  
  - Liquidity ratios: cash, current, quick, NWC-to-sales.[12]
  - Cash-flow ratios: cash flow to debt, to earnings, to turnover, and adequacy metrics.[12]
  - Profitability: ROA, ROE, ROCE, asset turnover, margin and return-based indicators.[12]
  - Leverage: debt ratio, debt-to-equity, interest coverage, equity multiplier.[12]
  - Shareholder and growth metrics: EPS, earnings yield, BVPS, headline EPS, turnover and assets growth.[12]

- **Valuation:**  
  - Explicit 5-year FCFF forecast with assumptions on revenue growth, EBITDA margin, tax, capex, and working capital.[12]
  - WACC and terminal growth anchored on South African risk premium, corporate tax rate, and GDP/inflation assumptions.[12]
  - Reconciliation from enterprise value to equity value and intrinsic value per share.[12]

## Equity Research Report Contents

The written report documents and interprets the modelling work, including:[11]

- Business overview and history, value chain, and geographic footprint.[11]
- Segment performance (Energy, Chemicals, Mining) and revenue mix.[11]
- Key macro and revenue drivers (Brent crude, ZAR/USD, chemical prices, domestic energy demand).[11]
- Sasol 2.0 transformation (cost optimisation, asset divestments, balance sheet repair) and progress on deleveraging and EBITDA uplift.[11]
- “Future Sasol” decarbonisation strategy, emissions reduction targets, and ESG positioning.[11]
- Governance and board composition, including executive and non-executive director profiles and incentive structure.[11]
- Competitive and strategic context using Porter’s Five Forces and PESTLE frameworks.[11]
- Final investment view with a 12‑month target price and BUY recommendation supported by DCF and relative valuation.[11][12]

## Skills Demonstrated

- End-to-end financial statement analysis and ratio construction for a JSE-listed company.[12]
- Building an integrated cash-flow and valuation model (FCFF, WACC, terminal value, sensitivity).[12]
- Equity research writing: structuring an investment thesis from both qualitative and quantitative evidence.[11][12]
- Application of strategic and ESG frameworks to complement valuation output.[11]
